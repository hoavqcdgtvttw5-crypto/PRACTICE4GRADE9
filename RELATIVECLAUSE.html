GRAMMAR SUMMARY: REDUCED RELATIVE CLAUSES

1. Active Reduction (V-ing)

When the verb in the relative clause is in the active voice, we reduce it by changing the verb to V-ing.

Relative Pronoun (who/which/that) + Verb (Active) 
→ Remove Pronoun + Change Verb to V-ing
Full: The man who is standing over there is my uncle.
Reduced: The man standing over there is my uncle.

2. Passive Reduction (V3/ed)

When the verb in the relative clause is in the passive voice, we reduce it by keeping only the Past Participle (V3/ed).

Relative Pronoun + be + Verb (V3/ed) 
→ Remove Pronoun & "be" + Keep V3/ed
Full: The bridge which was built in 1990 is very old.
Reduced: The bridge built in 1990 is very old.

3. To-infinitive Reduction

Used when the noun is modified by: the first, the second, the last, the only, the next or superlatives (the best, the tallest).

Noun + Relative Clause 
→ Noun + To + Verb (base form)
Full: Neil Armstrong was the first man who walked on the moon.
Reduced: Neil Armstrong was the first man to walk on the moon.

*Note: If the meaning is passive, use to be V3/ed (e.g., The only student to be punished).
